How your route was determined

KLARA looked at your symptoms, how urgent they are, how long they have lasted, where you are in the province, and which services are available. From that, it chose one recommended pathway—the option that best balances your safety with current capacity across the system.

Why this helps emergency care

Emergency departments need to stay available for life-threatening situations. When people with non-urgent needs use other services, it eases pressure on the ED so it can focus on true emergencies. In Nova Scotia, about 35% of emergency department visits are classified as non-emergency. Using the pathway KLARA suggests helps keep emergency care ready for those who need it most.

How you're helping Nova Scotia

Choosing the recommended pathway helps the system use existing services more effectively. KLARA does not create new demand; it helps direct people to the right place. That means better use of resources, less strain on overcrowded services, and improved access to care for everyone in the province. Your choice supports that.
